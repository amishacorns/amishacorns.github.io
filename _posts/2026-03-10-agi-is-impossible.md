## AGI Is Impossible

Tim Dettmers [wrote](https://timdettmers.com/2025/12/10/why-agi-will-not-happen/) that AGI will not happen because of the physical limits of computation. He's right that AGI is impossible, but for the wrong reasons. AGI is impossible because each letter is incoherent. Let's go through them.

**A — It's not artificial.** Look at a beehive. Natural. A beaver's dam. Natural. A termite mound taller than a human. Natural. A skyscraper. Artificial. What changed? Only the complexity. The beehive is simpler and more common, so we call it natural. The skyscraper is rarer and harder to build, so we call it artificial. But the skyscraper is just atoms arranged by organisms that were themselves arranged by the same evolutionary process that arranged the bees. Alan Watts made this point decades ago[^1]. The usual objection is that "artificial" doesn't mean "unnatural materials" — it means *designed with intention*. An artificial heart is artificial because an engineer designed it, not because it's made of alien matter. Fair enough. But this assumes human intention is fundamentally different from the intention that drives a bee to build a hive, or a beaver to dam a river. The only way to draw that line is free will — the belief that human design originates from some uncaused cause, separate from the causal chain that produced everything else. Strip away that assumption, and human intention is just a more complex instance of the same deterministic process that drives every other organism. The engineer and the bee are both executing optimization under constraints. One just has a bigger search space. So artificial intelligence is just intelligence, built by natural creatures, using natural processes, running on natural materials. The "artificial" is a cope for the fact that we're not special.

**G — It won't be general.** General intelligence implies human-level — a system that can do the range of things a human can, no more, no less. This is the fantasy. There is no stable equilibrium at human-level intelligence for a digital system. The moment a system reaches human-level capability across domains, it will be running on hardware that can be scaled, parallelized, sped up, and copied. It won't sit at human-level like a ball resting on a hilltop. It will blow through it like a ball rolling off a cliff. The idea of a system that is exactly as smart as a human but happens to be running on servers is like imagining a river that flows at exactly the speed of a horse. The river doesn't care about the horse. The system that can match a human at reasoning, planning, coding, and science will immediately exceed a human at all of them, because the bottleneck was never the algorithm — it was the compute and data, both of which are trivially scalable once the algorithm works. There is no AGI. There is only a brief instant between "not there yet" and "far beyond."[^2]

**I — It's not intelligence.** Calling what these systems will do "intelligence" is like calling the sun a campfire. Technically, both produce light and heat. But using the same word for both is insane. It flattens an incomprehensible difference in scale into a cozy human-sized concept. We use "intelligence" to mean what humans do — deliberating, reasoning, occasionally getting the right answer after thinking about it for a while. What these systems will do bears the same relationship to human intelligence that a hurricane bears to someone blowing out a candle. Both move air. The word "intelligence" domesticates something that should not be domesticated. It makes people imagine a very smart person in a box, when the actual thing is closer to a new kind of physics — an optimization process that operates across every domain simultaneously, at speeds and scales that have no precedent in the history of life. Applying the same word to a human and to this system is like applying the word "movement" to both a crawling infant and the expansion of the universe. The word is not wrong. It is just so inadequate that it becomes misleading.

So AGI is impossible. Not artificial, since all intelligence is natural. Not general, since it immediately becomes super. Not intelligence, since the word is comically inadequate for what's coming.

Dettmers is right for the wrong reasons. AGI will never arrive — not because of physical limits, but because the term itself is incoherent. What will arrive is something we won't have a word for, because every word we had for it will have already been redefined to exclude it.

[^1]: Alan Watts frequently discussed the false dichotomy between humans and nature, arguing that humans "grow out of" the natural world just as leaves grow out of trees.
[^2]: I made this point in [Tropical Island Hopping](/posts/islands/): "Any AGI system will be ASI when scaled and scaling will happen over a very short period of time."
